Let's Eat! A food tinder?

  • Collaborators: Jacqueline Shi | Sonya Zhang | Jessica Sun
  • Completed: March 16, 2018

    • After attending my first hackathon (Shehacks), Let's Eat was our team's stab at curing the indecisions we face when deciding where to eat in larger groups of people while encouraging consumers to choose to eat from more sustainable restaurants. Restaurants within our database are ranked based on the sustainability categories of locality, food waste and energy efficiency. With restaurants which are ranked higher in terms of sustainability, given that the users attend the restaurants they will collect "penne points" which could be redeemed at our participating restaurants for discounts.

    As complete beginners with coding, we hoped to code the very basic backend of the program and also design a prototype.

    We started with a quick user workflow diagram, illustrating the path of the user as they work through the app.

    After the user fills out their profile, they're instructed to create or join a group. Once the group is fully formed, each member of the group is prompted to fill our their preferences for the day. Based on the group preferences and the individual profiles of each member, the app suggest up 20 restaurants which the users can choose to swipe left or right. The chosen restaurant is based on the restaurant with the top swipes amongst the group.

    Reflection

    Overall, our team experience at Shehacks was amazing. We had our ups and downs throughout the 12 hours. Especially as beginners, the event was a great taste of the tech industry. In terms of where we could reiterate with our model here were the main critiques we came up with:

    • What if the user accidently swipes left on a resturant they didn't mean to?
    • What alternatives could be suggested given that the restaurants aren't appealing to the users?
    • How will you ensure that the rewards system with the penne points is an equitable evaluation?

    Thanks for reading our project! We're still learning and growing our skills!
