Thematic inspiration: Origins

  • Ongoing project

    • Looking back at my first drawings, I often chose to scratch geometric houses from waxy crayons and single hair stranded stick people representing my family because it was where I came from. Yet my inspiration as an artist today does not fall far. I am inspired to pay homage to the concept of Origins – the derivation or commencement of an existence. In this moment, I define visual arts as my second voice to appreciate the origins and stories of people and other artists that I find intriguing. My artwork also features an infusion of my cultural and self-developmental origins, being that they significantly influence my paradigm of existence and interpretation. In the curation of my portfolio, I strive to deliver a mixture of appreciating stylistic form and conceptual ideas in the exploration of impactfully retelling the story my chosen subject while speaking to subjects I value.


    I have developed as an artist, I began exploring unconventional media such as digital design and 3D pieces. The varying artists (including Francis Bacon, and Candy Jernigan) who have inspired specific pieces within my portfolio have largely diverging styles, yet an overlap in their mutual value of personal storytelling.

    Polyvinyl Chloride

    • Media: photography printed on acetate

    When prompted with the word: shiny, the theme of packaging and the thought of all things new, immediately came to mind. I specifically chose to focus on plastic material and ways to represent the material in an abstracted way. I wanted to present the subject such that the object and its reflective shadow practically becomes unidentifiable. This method of representation removes the common associations of everyday waste objects and challenges the viewers to search and interpret the material. The photographs focus on the concept that we live in a world of material waste. Our eyes are accustomed to packaging and plastic waste as it’s intently engrained into our everyday lifestyles. Through altering what we perceive as familiar, this photographic investigation challenges the viewers to re-evaluate disposable plastic waste.

    Initial inspiration

    Inspired by PepsiCo, we based our initial teaser draft on similar round fluid forms. Our colour scheme consisted of yellow and orange hues + bold and navy blues.

    Final Marketing Material

    After conducting market research, we note a falling trend in Facebook users amongst our chosen demographic of first and second year university students. Given this observation, our marketing campaign focused on capitalising on our instagram following and incorporating a video promotion aspect to engage viewers.
